Rohit Sharma, affectionately known as the "Hitman," is a prominent Indian cricketer celebrated for his elegant batting style and 
prolific run-scoring abilities. Born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, 
he has become one of the most distinguished figures in international cricket.​

Early Life and Domestic Career

Rohit's cricketing journey began in Mumbai's domestic circuit, 
where his exceptional talent quickly garnered attention. 
His performances for Mumbai in domestic tournaments paved the way for his inclusion in the national team.​

International Career Highlights

One-Day Internationals (ODIs): 
Rohit made his ODI debut in 2007. He holds the record for the highest individual score in an ODI innings, 
a monumental 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014. 
Notably, he is the only batsman to have scored three double centuries in ODIs. 
During the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup, he set a record by scoring five centuries in a single 
tournament, amassing a total of 648 runs.​

Test Cricket: Rohit's Test debut came in 2013. 
Initially batting in the middle order, 
he later transitioned to an opening role, 
where he achieved significant success, including a double century against South Africa in 2019.​

Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is): 
Rohit has been a stalwart in India's T20 setup. 
He captained the Indian team to victory in the 2024 ICC Men's T20 World Cup, 
after which he announced his retirement from T20 internationals on June 29, 2024.​


Indian Premier League (IPL) Career
In the IPL, Rohit has been synonymous with the Mumbai Indians franchise. 
As captain, he led the team to five titles in 2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, and 2020, 
making him one of the most successful captains in the tournament's history.​

Personal Life

Off the field, Rohit married Ritika Sajdeh on December 13, 2015. 
The couple welcomed their daughter, Samaira, on December 30, 2018. 
Beyond cricket, Rohit is an advocate for animal welfare, serving as an official Rhino Ambassador 
for WWF-India and supporting various initiatives with People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA).​

Recent Developments

As of March 2025, Rohit continues to lead the Indian cricket team in Test and ODI formats. 
He has expressed uncertainty about participating in the 2027 ODI World Cup, 
indicating that while retirement is not imminent, 
he remains undecided about his future in the longer term. ​
